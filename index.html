Feature-Optimized and Explainable Energy Consumption Prediction

"A feature-optimized and interpretable machine learning model using minimal environmental and temporal parameters can accurately predict short-term energy consumption across general residential and commercial buildings while maintaining computational efficiency."

Problem Statement

Energy consumption forecasting plays a crucial role in energy management, cost optimization, and sustainability planning. Existing prediction systems often rely on high-dimensional IoT sensor networks or computationally intensive deep learning models, limiting their deployment in small-scale or resource-constrained environments. There is a need for a lightweight, interpretable, and accurate energy consumption prediction framework that can operate effectively using a reduced set of environmental and temporal features.

Literature Review / Market Research

Research in energy forecasting commonly uses Linear Regression, Random Forest, Gradient Boosting, and LSTM networks. Datasets such as the Appliances Energy Prediction dataset from the UCI Machine Learning Repository are widely used for benchmarking. Literature shows deep learning models perform well for long-term forecasting but require higher computational resources, while ensemble methods provide strong performance with lower complexity. Limited focus exists on feature optimization and interpretability in energy prediction systems, and commercial energy management systems often prioritize accuracy without emphasizing decision transparency.

Research Gap / Innovation

Most existing energy consumption prediction models prioritize accuracy using complex architectures and large feature sets. However, limited research has explored feature reduction strategies for cost-efficient implementation, comparison between lightweight ML models and deep learning models under reduced feature conditions, explainable AI techniques for interpreting prediction outputs, and integration of environmental and temporal features in a simplified yet effective framework. This project proposes a feature-optimized, explainable, and computationally efficient energy prediction model suitable for broader residential and commercial applications.

System Methodology

Dataset / Input

Dataset source: Energy Consumption Prediction Dataset from Kaggle (https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/mrsimple07/energy-consumption-prediction). Description: time-based energy usage records with environmental and operational context for demand modeling. Input features include temperature, humidity, occupancy, HVAC usage, lighting usage, renewable energy contribution, and timestamp/datetime-derived fields (hour, day, weekday). Target variable: Energy Consumption (continuous numeric value representing energy used).

Model / Architecture

Linear Regression baseline, Random Forest Regressor, XGBoost Regressor, optional LSTM. Flow: collection -> cleaning -> feature engineering -> training -> evaluation -> prediction -> visualization.
